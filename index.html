Assessment 1 - Interaction Design Analysis

Information Design

In 7 Poems, the information is organised through a clear spatial layout and semantic labelling. Each poem title, such as “The Beetle” or “April Showers”, is displayed on a green bar that acts as both a navigation element and a thematic cue. This creates a relationship between the label and its content, supporting user orientation. Once clicked, the poem unfolds one word at a time in the centre of the textbox, creating a slow and rhyming information flow. The visual hierarchy is clear, with small titles and larger text placed in the centre. This helps guide the user’s attention naturally from selecting a poem to reading it.

However, Liquid Entropy presents a different design in UI, with a more ambiguous interface. A control panel in the top-left side includes sliders labelled with abstract terms such as “gleams”, “entropy” and “worm”. These lack a functional explanation and rely on users to experiment and interpret the results through audio-visual showing. Moreover, Liquid Entropy offers an immersive experience for users, but the guidance is limited.

Overall, 7 Poems gives clear structure and guidance through titles and green bars, while Liquid Entropy focuses on discovery through testing and does not give direct instruction.

Mapping

In 7 Poems, the spatial layout of the green bars creates a direct and intuitive connection between each interface element and its associated poem. Clicking a green bar reveals the text word by word alongside ambient audio, while clicking again pauses the sequence. This interaction reinforces user control. The mapping is clear, especially the position and function of each element, which correspond smoothly to its outcome.

By contrast, Liquid Entropy gives users a more abstract mapping. The control panel contains sliders with abstract labels like “gleams” and “entropy”, which give no immediate clues about their function. Users must guess and discover how each control affects the visuals and audio. While this mapping fosters creative exploration, it can also hinder usability for first-time users due to the lack of affordance and explanatory cues. The relationship between action and result is discoverable but not immediately apparent.

Finally, 7 Poems is easy to understand because the actions and results are connected. In contrast, Liquid Entropy uses a more experimental style, where the controls are less clear, encouraging users to explore and discover the results by themselves.

Characterisation

In 7 Poems, it uses a soft and yellow colour palette that creates a fresh and natural feeling, which matches the themes of the poems. The background is simple, allowing the text to stand out. When each word of the poem appears, it is matched with a gentle sound, like a musical note. This gives each word a sense of rhythm and turns reading into a sensory experience. The overall character feels calm, thoughtful, and connected to nature.

In contrast, Liquid Entropy uses a very different visual and sound style. Its main colours are black, grey and blue, which create a futuristic and almost sci-fi mood. The animation has a metallic texture, and the movement feels like the flow of energy or entropy. When the user adjusts the sliders, the visuals change in real time and are matched with metallic and bouncing sounds. The background audio is low and electronic, adding to the immersive, industrial feeling. The character of Liquid Entropy is experimental, bold and highly sensory.

Overall, the two interfaces create unique atmospheres by using different visual and audio choices. 7 Poems focuses on calm reflection, while Liquid Entropy creates an intense and futuristic experience.

Feedback

In 7 Poems, feedback is both visual and auditory, working together to confirm user actions and create a poetic atmosphere. Clicking a green bar expands a text box with the poem, signalling that the section is active. Words appear one by one with a musical note, creating a sense of rhythm like playing an instrument. If the text box is clicked again, the text and music pause, and the background colour turns green to indicate the paused state. Multiple poems can be opened at once, causing different melodies to overlap. This layered feedback enhances the sensory experience but also can make the soundscape more complex.

In Liquid Entropy, feedback is more exploratory. Sliding parameters in the control panel causes immediate changes to both visual and sound. The metallic textures shift in response, and electronic tones alter in pitch or intensity. These actions happen without guidance, so users must learn through exploration. In addition, the interface includes a “reset params” option, restoring default settings and encouraging repeated exploration.

Finally, compared to 7 Poems, where feedback is clear and state changes are visually marked, Liquid Entropy focus on sensory immersion and encourages user-driven exploration.
